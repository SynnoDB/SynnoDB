Performance optimization pass.

Current end-to-end runtime: `${current_total_runtime_s}` seconds. Your task is to improve performance while preserving correctness for all queries.

Prioritize reducing bytes read from storage and improving read throughput. Any optimization must be justified by its expected impact on end-to-end runtime.

Focus areas:
1. **Read less data**

   Minimize the amount of data read from disk.

   Consider:

   * Precomputing query-relevant results at the RowGroup and DataPage level, such as `sum`, `avg`, `count`, or other aggregates required by the workload.
   * Adding or improving metadata for filtering and pruning, such as min/max, bloom filters, dictionaries, zone maps, value sketches, or workload-specific indexes.
   * Improving predicate pushdown so that irrelevant RowGroups, pages, or columns are skipped as early as possible.
   * Choosing encodings that reduce bytes-on-disk and bytes-read-per-query without adding excessive decode overhead.

2. **Use faster lightweight encodings**

   Prefer lightweight, SIMD-friendly, and fast-decoding encodings over heavyweight general-purpose compression when appropriate.

   Consider:

   * FastLanes-style integer/vector encodings.
   * ALP-style floating-point compression.
   * FSST-style string compression.
   * Dictionary, RLE, bit-packing, delta, frame-of-reference, or hybrid encodings.
   * Reducing unnecessary use of heavyweight compression such as Zstd when decompression cost hurts runtime.

3. **Read data faster**

   Optimize the I/O path and maximize useful bandwidth.

   Consider:

   * Measuring or estimating whether the workload is I/O-bound, decode-bound, or compute-bound.
   * Improving sequential read patterns and reducing small random reads where possible.
   * Aligning physical layout with query access patterns.
   * Increasing effective I/O bandwidth through batching, prefetching, larger reads, or asynchronous reads.

4. **Overlap I/O and computation**

   Try to keep both storage and CPU fully utilized.

   Consider:

   * Pipelining page reads, decompression/decoding, filtering, and aggregation.
   * Using asynchronous I/O or prefetching to hide storage latency.
   * Processing already-read data while the next pages are being fetched.
   * Avoiding pipeline stalls caused by blocking reads or serial decode stages.

For every proposed optimization, explain:

* What changes in the file format, metadata, encoding, or execution path.
* Why correctness is preserved.
* Why it should reduce runtime.
* Whether the improvement mainly comes from reading fewer bytes, reading faster, decoding faster, pruning better, or overlapping I/O and computation.
